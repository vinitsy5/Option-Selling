Chapter 1: Options Fundamentals

What is an Option?

An option is a contract that gives you the right (but not obligation) to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price (strike) before or at expiration.

üîë Key Point: Options are WASTING ASSETS - they lose value over time (time decay). Unlike stocks, if you do nothing, options expire worthless!

Max profit (buying) Unlimited Strike - Premium
Max loss (buying) Premium paid Premium paid
Max profit (selling) Premium received Premium received
Max loss (selling) Unlimited (naked) Strike - Premium

Option Premium Components

THE FUNDAMENTAL FORMULA:

Option Premium = Intrinsic Value + Time Value

1Ô∏è‚É£ Intrinsic Value - The "real" value if exercised NOW

  • CALL: Max(0, Current Price - Strike)
  • PUT: Max(0, Strike - Current Price)
  • OTM options have ZERO intrinsic value
  • This part never decays (moves 1:1 with underlying)

2Ô∏è‚É£ Time Value - The "hope" premium for future profit

  • Reflects probability of becoming profitable
  • DECAYS TO ZERO at expiration (theta decay)
  • Highest for ATM options (maximum uncertainty)
  • Affected by: Time remaining, volatility, interest rates
  • This is what you're trading in calendar spreads!

üìä VEGA - Volatility Sensitivity

Understanding IV (Implied Volatility):

  • IV = Market's expectation of future price volatility
  • High IV = Market expects big moves = Options expensive
  • Low IV = Market expects calm = Options cheap
  • IV can change instantly on news/events
