Chapter 1: Options Fundamentals

What is an Option?

An option is a contract that gives you the right (but not obligation) to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price (strike) before or at expiration.

🔑 Key Point: Options are WASTING ASSETS - they lose value over time (time decay). Unlike stocks, if you do nothing, options expire worthless!

CALL vs PUT Options - Complete Comparison

Aspect CALL Option PUT Option
Right to BUY the underlying asset SELL the underlying asset
When you buy BULLISH (expect price ↑) BEARISH (expect price ↓)
When you sell BEARISH or neutral BULLISH or neutral
Profit if Price goes ABOVE strike Price goes BELOW strike
Max profit (buying) Unlimited Strike - Premium
Max loss (buying) Premium paid Premium paid
Max profit (selling) Premium received Premium received
Max loss (selling) Unlimited (naked) Strike - Premium

Option Premium Components

THE FUNDAMENTAL FORMULA:

Option Premium = Intrinsic Value + Time Value

1️⃣ Intrinsic Value - The "real" value if exercised NOW

  • CALL: Max(0, Current Price - Strike)
  • PUT: Max(0, Strike - Current Price)
  • OTM options have ZERO intrinsic value
  • This part never decays (moves 1:1 with underlying)

2️⃣ Time Value - The "hope" premium for future profit

  • Reflects probability of becoming profitable
  • DECAYS TO ZERO at expiration (theta decay)
  • Highest for ATM options (maximum uncertainty)
  • Affected by: Time remaining, volatility, interest rates
  • This is what you're trading in calendar spreads!
💰 Premium Breakdown Example:

Scenario: BTC at ₹70,000, 7 days to expiry

₹70k CALL (ATM):
Premium: ₹2,500
Intrinsic: ₹0 (ATM has no intrinsic value)
Time Value: ₹2,500 (100% is time value!)
→ This ₹2,500 will decay to ₹0 in 7 days if price doesn't move!

₹65k CALL (ITM):
Premium: ₹6,000
Intrinsic: ₹5,000 (70k - 65k)

→ Only ₹1,000 decays, ₹5,000 stays as long as BTC > ₹65k

₹75k CALL (OTM):
Premium: ₹800
Intrinsic: ₹0
Time Value: ₹800 (100% time value)
→ Entire premium will likely decay to ₹0!

Chapter 2: The Greeks - Your Risk Dashboard

🔺 DELTA - Directional Risk & Probability

Delta = Change in option price ÷ Change in underlying price
Range: 0 to +1 for CALLs | 0 to -1 for PUTs

What Delta Tells You:

  • Directional Exposure: How much your option moves with the underlying
  • Probability: Delta ≈ probability of expiring ITM
  • Hedge Ratio: How many shares equivalent you're holding
Delta Value Meaning (CALL) Probability ITM Price Sensitivity Use Case
0.90 - 1.00 Deep ITM ~90-100% Moves almost 1:1 with stock Stock replacement
0.70 - 0.80 Moderately ITM ~70-80% ₹70-80 per ₹100 move Directional trades
~0.50 ATM ~50% ₹50 per ₹100 move Calendar spreads!
0.30 - 0.40 Slightly OTM ~30-40% ₹30-40 per ₹100 move Credit spreads
0.10 - 0.20 Far OTM ~10-20% ₹10-20 per ₹100 move Lottery tickets
< 0.10 Very far OTM < 10% Barely moves Usually worthless
📈 Delta in Action:

Scenario: BTC at ₹70,000
You buy ₹70k CALL with Delta 0.50
Premium paid: ₹2,500

BTC moves to ₹71,000 (+₹1,000):
Your option should gain: ₹1,000 × 0.50 = ₹500
New premium: ~₹3,000
Your profit: ₹500 (20% gain!)

BTC moves to ₹69,000 (-₹1,000):
Your option loses: ₹1,000 × 0.50 = ₹500
New premium: ~₹2,000
Your loss: ₹500 (20% loss)
🎯 Calendar Spread Delta Strategy:
• Short leg: Delta ~0.50 (ATM)
• Long leg: Delta ~0.50 (ATM)
• Net Delta: ~0.00 (market neutral!)
• You don't care about direction, just time decay difference!

⏰ THETA - Time Decay (The Calendar Spread's Best Friend)

Theta = Change in option price per day passing
Negative for buyers | Positive for sellers

The Harsh Reality of Theta:

  • Every day that passes, your option loses value (if you bought it)
  • Decay is NON-LINEAR - accelerates as expiration approaches
  • ATM options have the HIGHEST theta (most time value to lose)
  • Weekends count as 2-3 days of decay!
Days to Expiry Daily Decay Rate Weekly Loss Example (₹2000 option) Strategy
60+ DTE ~1-2% per day ~7-14% Loses ₹20-40/day Buy for calendars
30-60 DTE ~2-3% per day ~14-21% Loses ₹40-60/day Swing trades
15-30 DTE ~3-5% per day ~21-35% Loses ₹60-100/day Calendar LONG leg
7-15 DTE ~5-10% per day ~35-70% Loses ₹100-200/day Credit spreads
3-7 DTE ~10-20% per day ~70-100%+ Loses ₹200-400/day Calendar SHORT leg!
0-3 DTE ~30-50% per day Expires! Loses ₹600-1000/day 0DTE scalping only

🔥 Last 7 days = 47.5% of total value lost!
🔥 Last 2 days = 25% of remaining value lost!
💰 THE CALENDAR SPREAD THETA EDGE:

Setup:
• SHORT 3 DTE option: Theta = -₹300/day (loses fast!)

Why it works:
• You collect ₹300/day from short option decay
• You lose only ₹80/day from long option decay
• Net: ₹220/day profit if price stays near strike!
• This is your EDGE in calendar spreads!

📊 VEGA - Volatility Sensitivity

Vega = Change in option price per 1% change in IV
Always positive (higher IV = higher premium)

Understanding IV (Implied Volatility):

  • IV = Market's expectation of future price volatility
  • High IV = Market expects big moves = Options expensive
  • Low IV = Market expects calm = Options cheap
  • IV can change instantly on news/events
IV Change Effect on Premium Winner Loser Example
IV +10% Premium increases Option buyers
Long vega		 Option sellers
Short vega		 Earnings announcement coming
IV -10% Premium decreases Option sellers
Short vega		 Option buyers
Long vega		 "IV crush" after earnings
💥 Vega Example - The IV Crush:

Before Earnings:
BTC at ₹70,000
₹70k CALL, 7 DTE
IV: 80% (high - market expects volatility)
Vega: 30
Premium: ₹3,000

After Earnings (price unchanged):
BTC still at ₹70,000
IV: 40% (dropped 40%!)
Premium loss from IV: 40% × 30 = ₹1,200 loss!
New Premium: ~₹1,800

Result: Lost 40% even though price didn't move!
This is "IV crush" - kills option buyers after events!
📈 Calendar Spread Vega Profile:

Short-term options: Low vega (less time for volatility to matter)
Long-term options: High vega (more time = volatility matters more)

Net Result: Calendar spreads are LONG VEGA
• You PROFIT if IV increases
• You LOSE if IV decreases sharply

Best Practice:
• Enter when IV is LOW (cheap to buy)
• Exit when IV SPIKES (expensive to close)
• Avoid trading right after earnings (IV crush risk)

🔄 GAMMA - Rate of Delta Change

Gamma = Change in Delta per ₹1 change in underlying
Highest for ATM options near expiry

Why Gamma Matters:

  • Shows how FAST your delta changes
  • High gamma = Delta changes rapidly (risky for sellers!)
  • Low gamma = Delta stays stable (safer)
  • ATM options on expiry day have EXTREME gamma
🎢 Gamma Risk - The Expiry Day Nightmare:

You sold ATM 70k CALL on expiry day (0 DTE)
BTC at ₹70,000
Premium collected: ₹500
Current Delta: 0.50
Gamma: 0.15 (VERY HIGH!)

BTC moves to ₹70,500 (+₹500):
New Delta: 0.50 + (0.15 × 0.5) = 0.575
Loss so far: ~₹288

BTC continues to ₹71,000 (+₹500 more):
New Delta: 0.575 + (0.15 × 0.5) = 0.65
Additional loss: ~₹325
Total loss: ₹613 (you only collected ₹500!)

BTC reaches ₹71,500 (+₹500 more):
Delta now ~0.80 (accelerating!)
Loss keeps growing faster!

This is GAMMA RISK - losses accelerate!
Calendar Spread Gamma:
• Short leg: High gamma (scary near expiry!)
• Long leg: Low gamma (stable)
• Risk: If price moves away, short gamma hurts more than long gamma helps
• This is why you EXIT if price moves >7% away from strike!

Position Size = (Account Risk % × Account Size) ÷ Max Loss per Contract

Position = (0.03 × ₹5,00,000) ÷ ₹500
= ₹15,000 ÷ ₹500
= 30 contracts max
